Using our data from Global Fishing Watch, a program that focuses on expanding fisheries transparency and understanding the behaviors of commercial fishing vessels, we constructed the following visualizations that show the locations of areas being fished, as well as the time spent at those locations. After observing our data, we found that the peak of greatest number of fishing hours was around 2014. Hours spent using drifting longlines (a fishing practice where lines drift on the surface and are attached to baited hooks) was found to be gradually decreasing, although hours spent on pots & traps (a fishing practice where animals are trapped in metal/wood cages) seem to be increasing. We hope to spend the rest of the quarter learning about the shift in fishing methods.
